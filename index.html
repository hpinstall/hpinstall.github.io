Your Complete Beginner's Roadmap to HP Printer Setup via 123.hp.com Install

New printer, new box, a dozen cables — and no idea where to start? This guide walks you through the entire 123.hp.com install process step by step, so your HP printer is unboxed, connected, and printing in under twenty minutes.

Introduction

Why 123.hp.com is the only place you need to start

Setting up a new printer used to mean digging through a driver CD that nobody owns a CD drive for anymore. HP fixed that with 123.hp.com — a single web address that detects your printer model, downloads the correct driver, and walks you through Wi-Fi setup automatically.

Whether you've just unboxed an HP DeskJet, OfficeJet, ENVY, LaserJet, or Smart Tank printer, the 123.hp.com install wizard is built for people who have never set up a printer before. No technical background needed — just your printer, your computer or phone, and a working internet connection.

Quick tip: Keep your printer powered on and within reach of your Wi-Fi router before you begin. Most setup hiccups happen simply because the printer is too far from the signal.

Setting Up Your HP Printer, Step by Step

From unboxing to your first printed page

1Unbox and power on the printer

Remove all packing tape, foam inserts, and the strip of plastic film covering the control panel. Plug in the power cable and press the power button. Let the printer fully initialize — it will usually prompt you on its own screen to choose a language and country.

2Install the ink or toner cartridges

Open the cartridge access door, remove the orange tape and caps from each cartridge, and slot them in until you hear a click. Close the door and let the printer run its automatic alignment page — this is normal and only takes a minute.

3Go to 123.hp.com on your browser

On the computer or phone you'll be printing from, open a browser and type 123.hp.com directly into the address bar — not a search engine. The site will ask for your printer's model number, found on a sticker on the front or inside the cartridge door.

4Download HP Smart or the setup software

123.hp.com will detect your operating system and offer the correct download — usually the HP Smart app for Windows, Mac, iOS, or Android. Download and open it once it finishes installing.

5Connect your printer to Wi-Fi

The setup software will scan for nearby printers. Select yours from the list, then choose your home Wi-Fi network and enter the password when prompted. The printer's display will confirm once it's successfully connected.

6Print a test page

Once setup finishes, the app will offer to print a test page. Do this — it confirms the driver, Wi-Fi connection, and ink levels are all working correctly before you move on to your actual documents.

Stuck along the way? Restarting both the printer and your router resolves the majority of connection failures during 123.hp.com install. Try this before anything else.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common 123.hp.com install questions

What is 123.hp.com used for?
It's HP's official setup portal that identifies your printer model and provides the correct drivers and software, including the HP Smart app, so you can install and connect your printer without searching for files manually.
Do I need to create an HP account?
An account isn't required to install your printer, but signing up does let you register your warranty, order ink, and manage multiple printers from one dashboard later on.
My printer isn't showing up during setup. What should I do?
Make sure the printer and your computer or phone are on the same Wi-Fi network, then restart the printer. If it still doesn't appear, try moving the printer closer to the router and run the search again.
Can I set up my printer using just my phone?
Yes. The HP Smart app is available for both iOS and Android, and it supports the full setup process — driver installation, Wi-Fi connection, and test printing — without needing a computer at all.
Is 123.hp.com install free?
Yes, the setup software and drivers from 123.hp.com are completely free. You only pay for ink, toner, or any optional HP+ subscription services you choose to add later.
